A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam , born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a distinguished scientist and statesman. He played a vital role in India's space and missile programs, earning him the title "Missile Man of India." Serving as the 11th President from 2002 to 2007, he focused on inspiring youth and promoting education. Kalam authored numerous books and delivered motivational speeches, leaving a profound impact on people's lives. His contributions to nuclear technology, including the successful Pokhran-II tests, showcased India's scientific prowess. Kalam's humility, simplicity, and dedication earned him the respect and admiration of people worldwide. Awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1997, his legacy continues to inspire future generations to pursue innovation and excellence. He passed away on July 27, 2015, but his vision for a prosperous and developed India lives on.
